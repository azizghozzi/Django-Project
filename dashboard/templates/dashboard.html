Covid overview

Coronaviruses (CoV) are a large family of viruses that cause illnesses ranging from the common cold to illnesses more serious such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV) and severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARSCoV). A new coronavirus (nCoV) corresponds to a new strain that has not been identified in humans previously

Coronaviruses are zoonotic in type, that is, they are transmitted from animals to humans. Detailed investigations have revealed that SARS-CoV and MERS-CoV were transmitted to humans by civet cats and dromedaries respectively. Several known coronaviruses circulate in animals which have not yet infected humans.

Common signs of infection are symptoms breathing, fever, cough, shortness of breath and difficulty respiratory. In the most severe cases, the infection can cause pneumonia, severe acute respiratory syndrome, kidney failure and even death. Standard recommendations for preventing the spread of infection include regular hand washing, washing cover the mouth and nose when coughing and sneezing, cooking full of meat and eggs. Avoid close contact with anyone with symptoms of respiratory illness such as coughing and sneezing.
